Valuepay Payment Gateway — Integration & KYC Requirements

Prepared for: Nakowa Agricultural Supplies Website: nakowa.com.ng Date: May 2026
1. Overview

The Nakowa AIOMS (Agricultural Inventory & Operations Management System) is integrated with Valuepay as its online payment gateway. Valuepay enables Nakowa customers to pay for orders via card, bank transfer, USSD, and QR code — directly from the website.

To go live with online payments, a verified Valuepay merchant account must be created for Nakowa. This document outlines everything needed: KYC documents, technical configuration, and dashboard settings.

Contact Valuepay Valuepay is operated by Konplit Technologies. To begin merchant onboarding, contact them at valuepay.konplit.com or reach out via their support channels. Mention that you need a merchant account for an agricultural e-commerce and inventory management platform.
2. KYC (Know Your Customer) Documents

Nigerian payment processors are regulated by the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) and require KYC verification before activating a merchant account. The following documents will be requested during onboarding.

2.1 — Business / Company Documents

Document Required Details
CAC Certificate of Incorporation Required Certificate issued by the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) showing the business is registered in Nigeria. Include the RC number.
CAC Status Report (Form CAC 1.1) Required Current status report showing directors, shareholders, and registered office address. Obtainable from the CAC portal or a CAC-accredited agent.
Memorandum & Articles of Association (MEMART) Required For registered companies (Ltd/PLC). Not required for sole proprietorships or partnerships — submit Business Name registration instead.
Business Name Registration Certificate If applicable Required if the business operates as a sole proprietorship or partnership under a business name, rather than a limited company.
Tax Identification Number (TIN) Required Issued by the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS). Obtainable online at tin.jtb.gov.ng or at a FIRS office.
Board Resolution / Directors' Resolution Required A formal resolution from the board of directors authorising the company to open a payment gateway account and nominating the signatory. Must be on company letterhead, signed, and dated.
SCUML Certificate If applicable Special Control Unit against Money Laundering certificate. Required for businesses in designated non-financial businesses and professions (DNFBPs). Agricultural commodity trading businesses may be required to register. Check with your legal adviser.

2.2 — Director / Business Owner Personal Documents

Required for each director, major shareholder (≥5% ownership), or the primary account signatory.

Document Required Details
Valid Government-Issued ID Required Any one of the following (must be valid and not expired):
  • National Identity Card (NIN Slip / ID Card)
  • International Passport (data page)
  • Driver's Licence
  • Permanent Voter's Card (PVC)
BVN (Bank Verification Number) Required BVN of each director / signatory. This is verified electronically against NIBSS records. Dial *565*0# on your registered phone to retrieve your BVN.
NIN (National Identification Number) Required 11-digit NIN for each director. Dial *346# to retrieve your NIN.
Passport Photograph Required Recent colour passport photograph (white background, plain clothing) for each director / signatory. Soft copy (JPG/PNG) acceptable for online submission.
Proof of Address Required Any one dated within the last 3 months:
  • Utility bill (electricity, water, DSTV)
  • Bank statement (with address printed)
  • Government-issued address letter

2.3 — Bank Account & Financial Documents

Document Required Details
Business Bank Account Details Required Account name, account number, and bank name of the business's Nigerian bank account. Settlements from Valuepay will be paid into this account.
3–6 Months Bank Statement Required Bank statement for the business account showing recent transactions. Must be stamped and signed by the bank (or downloaded from internet banking as an official PDF).
Voided Cheque or Account Confirmation Letter Optional Some processors request a voided cheque or a letter from the bank confirming the account details, for settlement verification purposes.

2.4 — Business Verification Documents

Document Required Details
Business Address Proof Required Utility bill or tenancy/lease agreement for the business premises at Anguwan Jaba, Lafia, Nasarawa State — dated within 3 months.
Photographs of Business Premises Optional Some processors request photos of the shop front, interior, and signage to verify the business is operational.
Website / App Description Required Brief description of how payments are collected (e.g. customers place orders on nakowa.com.ng, pay via Valuepay checkout, goods are delivered or collected). Include the live website URL.
Document Preparation Tips All documents should be clear, legible, and in PDF or high-resolution JPG/PNG format. Physical documents must be scanned — do not submit photos taken with poor lighting. Ensure all documents are in English or accompanied by a certified translation.
3. Technical Configuration

Once the merchant account is approved, Valuepay will provide API credentials. The developer (or hosting team) must configure the following in the server environment.

3.1 — API Credentials (from Valuepay Dashboard)

Environment Variable Required Where to Find
VALUEPAY_PRIVATE_KEY Required Valuepay Merchant Dashboard → Settings → API Keys → Private Key. Starts with KP_. Used server-side only — never expose in browser.
VALUEPAY_ENCRYPTION_KEY Required Valuepay Merchant Dashboard → Settings → API Keys → Encryption Key. Starts with KP_ENC_. Used to verify that webhook events genuinely originate from Valuepay.
VALUEPAY_BASE_URL Pre-set Default: https://valuepay.konplit.com/v1. Leave unchanged unless Valuepay explicitly provides a different API endpoint.
NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL Required The live domain of the Nakowa website (e.g. https://nakowa.com.ng). This is used to automatically generate the correct webhook and redirect URLs for Valuepay.

3.2 — URLs to Register in Valuepay Dashboard

After logging into the Valuepay merchant dashboard, navigate to Settings → Webhooks / Notifications and add both URLs below. Replace nakowa.com.ng with the actual live domain when it is deployed.

Webhook URL (Payment Notification Endpoint)
https://nakowa.com.ng/api/webhooks/valuepay

Valuepay will POST a notification to this URL whenever a customer's payment is completed, failed, or reversed. The system uses this to automatically mark orders as paid. Enable the transaction.completed event (and optionally transaction.failed).

Payment Return / Redirect URL
https://nakowa.com.ng/payment/return

After a customer completes (or cancels) payment on the Valuepay checkout page, they are redirected back to this URL. The system then verifies the transaction and shows the customer a confirmation.

Important — Live Domain Required Valuepay cannot send webhooks to localhost or local development environments. Both URLs must use the live, publicly accessible domain (e.g. https://nakowa.com.ng) and must support HTTPS. Ensure SSL/TLS is active on the domain before going live.
4. Merchant Onboarding Steps
  1. Register on the Valuepay Merchant Portal Visit valuepay.konplit.com, click "Create Account" / "Become a Merchant", and complete the registration form with the business name, email, and phone number.
  2. Submit KYC Documents Upload all required documents from Section 2 of this document. Ensure files are clear and below the portal's maximum file size (usually 5 MB per file).
  3. Await Account Verification Valuepay's compliance team reviews the documents. This typically takes 2–5 business days. You may be contacted for additional documents or clarification.
  4. Receive API Credentials Once verified, your merchant dashboard will show your Private Key and Encryption Key under Settings → API Keys. Copy both keys and send them securely to the developer for configuration.
  5. Developer Configures the System The developer adds the API keys to the server environment and registers the webhook and redirect URLs in the Valuepay dashboard (as specified in Section 3.2).
  6. Test Mode Verification Use Valuepay's test/sandbox mode to place a test order and verify the full payment flow works — from checkout to order confirmation. The developer will confirm this.
  7. Go Live Switch the system from test mode to live mode by replacing test keys with live keys. Online payments are now active on nakowa.com.ng.
5. Settlement & Fees

The following are standard Valuepay terms — confirm exact rates with Valuepay during onboarding as they may negotiate rates based on business volume.

Settlement Timeline

  • Card & USSD: T+1 business day
  • Bank Transfer: Same day or T+1
  • Settlement is to the registered business bank account
  • Settlement reports available in the merchant dashboard

Typical Fee Structure

  • Transaction fee: ~1.5% per transaction
  • Cap: usually ₦2,000 per transaction
  • International cards: higher rate (~3.9%)
  • Confirm exact rates with Valuepay
Tip — Charge Customers the Fee The system can be configured to add the payment gateway fee on top of the order total so that Nakowa does not bear the transaction cost. Discuss this with the developer if you want this enabled.
